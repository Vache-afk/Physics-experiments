Throwing Ball from Cliff Experiment

Position Equations (Projectile Motion):

Horizontal Position: \( x(t) = v_0 \cos(\theta) \cdot t \)

Vertical Position: \( y(t) = h_0 + v_0 \sin(\theta) \cdot t - \frac{1}{2} g t^2 \)

These equations describe the ball's position at any time t.
Horizontal motion is constant velocity, while vertical motion includes gravity's effect.

Velocity Equations:

Horizontal Velocity: \( v_x = v_0 \cos(\theta) \) (remains constant)

Vertical Velocity: \( v_y(t) = v_0 \sin(\theta) - g \cdot t \) (changes over time)

Horizontal velocity stays constant as there's no horizontal acceleration.
Vertical velocity decreases due to gravity acting downward.

Key Results:

Time of Flight: \( t_{flight} = \frac{v_0 \sin(\theta) + \sqrt{(v_0 \sin(\theta))^2 + 2 g h_0}}{g} \)

Maximum Height: \( y_{max} = h_0 + \frac{(v_0 \sin(\theta))^2}{2g} \)

Horizontal Range: \( R = v_0 \cos(\theta) \cdot t_{flight} \)

Time of flight is when the ball hits the ground (y=0).
Max height occurs at the peak of the trajectory.
Range is the horizontal distance traveled.

Variables Explained:
  • \( v_0 \): Initial speed (m/s) - how fast the ball is thrown
  • \( \theta \): Launch angle (degrees) - angle from horizontal
  • \( h_0 \): Initial height (m) - height of the cliff
  • \( g \): Gravity (9.81 m/s²) - downward acceleration
  • \( t \): Time (s) - time since launch
  • \( x(t) \): Horizontal position (m) - distance from cliff
  • \( y(t) \): Vertical position (m) - height above ground
  • \( v_x \): Horizontal velocity (m/s) - constant speed rightward
  • \( v_y(t) \): Vertical velocity (m/s) - changes with time
