Throwing Ball from Cliff Experiment

The position of the ball is calculated using the following equations:

Horizontal Position: \[ x = v_{x0} t \]

Vertical Position: \[ y = h_0 + v_{y0} t - \frac{1}{2} g t^2 \]

Horizontal Velocity: \[ v_x = v_{x0} \]

Vertical Velocity: \[ v_y = v_{y0} - g t \]

Where \( x \) and \( y \) are the horizontal and vertical positions, \( v_x \) and \( v_y \) are the horizontal and vertical velocities, \( v_{x0} \) and \( v_{y0} \) are the initial horizontal and vertical velocities, \( h_0 \) is the cliff height, \( g \) is the gravitational acceleration (9.81 m/s²), and \( t \) is time.
